Organisation Profile
Who is SAUS?

The South African Union of Students (SAUS) is the official national representative body for students in higher education across South Africa. Launched in April 2006, SAUS unites the voices of students from all 26 public universities and colleges, ensuring their concerns are amplified at a national level.

SAUS is driven by the belief that education is a right, not a privilege — with a mission to ensure that every student, regardless of background, has access to affordable, quality, and inclusive education.

Logo Usage

The SAUS logo features a globe centred on Africa, flanked by raised fists in the national colours — symbolising student power, continental awareness, and pan-African unity. The circular design with gold stars echoes the African Union aesthetic.

  • Always use the logo on a white or very light background for maximum clarity.
  • Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "S" in SAUS around all sides.
  • The circular logo mark may be used standalone as a favicon and app icon.
  • Do not distort, recolour, or apply drop shadows to the logo.
  • Do not place the logo on dark or busy photographic backgrounds without a white container.

Website Goals
What the Website Must Achieve
🏛️

Build Credibility & Authority

Position SAUS as the definitive, legitimate voice of students in South African higher education. The site must convey professionalism and institutional weight.

📢

Share Information & News

Serve as the go-to source for students, media, and institutions to find SAUS positions, press releases, campaigns, and announcements.

🙋

Enable Student Participation

Make it easy for students to get involved — understand their rights, engage with campaigns, contact leadership, and access resources.

🔗

Connect SRCs to National Body

Provide a transparent window into SAUS governance — NEC members, congress resolutions, and institutional affiliations across all 26 universities.

📲

Mobile-First Accessibility

The majority of South African students access the internet via mobile. The website must be responsive, fast, and fully functional on all device sizes.

About Page
Organisation backstory, mission, vision, values

Company Story: SAUS was officially launched in April 2006 as the first national union of students since NUSAS dissolved in the late 1980s. It was formed to bring together two wings of higher education — universities and universities of technology — under one unified student voice. The institutional restructuring by the South African government in the early 2000s resulted in a new higher education sector which necessitated a national student body. Students have historically played an important role in education since the 1960s and the 1976 uprisings. The 9th National Elective Congress was held in 2025 at Nelson Mandela University and Kimberley, Northern Cape.

MissionTo consolidate and strengthen students' views in the governance and enhancement of Higher Education and Training in South Africa.
VisionA unified, equitable, non-sexist, non-racial, democratic education system appropriate and responsive to the needs of South Africa, Africa, and the world.
C
Campaigns Page
Active advocacy issues — must feel urgent and action-oriented
  • NSFAS Reform — Redesigning NSFAS for sustainability and reliability with a strengthened communication system.
  • Student Debt Relief — Campaigning for government to clear all historical student debt, starting with deceased students and unemployed graduates.
  • Missing Middle Funding — Developing a sustainable funding scheme for "missing middle," postgraduate, and N+ students.
  • No Application Fees — Abolishing application fees at all higher education institutions.
  • GBV & Campus Safety — Proposing minimum standards for security and handling GBVF cases on campuses.
  • AI in Education — Developing a working paper on the role of AI in broadening academic support for students.
  • National Credit Act — Opposing amendments that would list student debt with credit bureaus.

Design System
Visual Direction & Style

Design Principles

  • White Space First — Generous padding and breathing room. Never cluttered.
  • Colour with Purpose — Use brand colours as accents, not wallpaper. White dominates.
  • Bold Typography — Headlines are large and confident. Body text is clean and readable.
  • Mobile-First — Design for 360px wide first; scale up for desktop.
  • Fast & Accessible — No heavy animations. WCAG AA contrast. Screen-reader friendly.
